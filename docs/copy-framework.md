# Copy Framework

## Brand voice rules
- Write short, clean sentences
- Keep language human and sharp
- Build trust quickly
- Do not use buzzwords or agency clichés
- Every line should either clarify value, show proof, or move the visitor toward action

## Homepage

### Hero
Goal: immediate clarity and mood.

Formula:
- headline that frames the value
- one-line positioning statement
- CTA to view work
- CTA to start a project

Example direction:
- Films with a clear point of view.
- Cinematic video production for brands, nonprofits, churches, and events that need real story, not filler.

### Trust strip
Goal: quiet proof.

Include:
- award mention
- notable clients
- years of experience
- film count or project count
- FAA drone certification if applicable

### Positioning section
Goal: explain the advantage of one filmmaker.

Key ideas:
- direct communication
- consistency of vision
- faster decision-making
- leaner process
- less handoff, more clarity

### Featured work
Goal: make the work feel editorial and premium.

Each card should include:
- project title
- category
- one-line outcome or angle
- cinematic still

### Services
Goal: explain outcomes, not just offerings.

Structure for each service:
- what it is
- who it is for
- what problem it solves
- likely deliverables
- why this process works better

### About preview
Goal: show the founder behind the work.

Include:
- short founder story
- Oklahoma-rooted credibility
- story-first approach
- multi-disciplinary capability

### Final CTA
Goal: reduce friction.

Suggested line:
- Let’s make something worth watching.

Support with:
- short reassurance sentence
- form or direct contact options

## Services page
For each service, lead with the client problem and the finished outcome.

Example structure:
1. What this service is
2. Who it is for
3. What it solves
4. What deliverables may include
5. Why Bonus Creative’s approach works

## About page
Write this page like a founder introduction, not a résumé dump.

Suggested flow:
- opening paragraph
- how the filmmaker works
- what kinds of stories he is drawn to
- industries served
- why direct collaboration matters
- award or credibility block

## Contact page
Keep the copy clean and welcoming.

Example direction:
- Tell me what you’re making, when you need it, and what matters most. I’ll follow up personally.
